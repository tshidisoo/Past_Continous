PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

  • The past continuous tense (also called the past progressive tense) describes actions that were ongoing at a specific moment in the past or were interrupted by another action.
  • It is formed using the past tense of the verb "to be" (was/were) + present participle (verb + -ing).

Formation of the Past Continuous Tense

Affirmative Sentences

Subject + was/were + verb-ing
Pronoun Was/Were + verb-ing
Iwas reading
Youwere reading
He/She/Itwas reading
Wewere reading
Youwere reading
Theywere reading

Verb => read

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Examples

  • I was writing a book yesterday.
  • She was talking on the phone when I arrived.
  • They were watching a movie all night.

Formation of the Past Continuous Tense

Negative Sentences

Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing
Pronoun Was/Were + not + verb-ing
Iwas not taking
Youwere not taking
He/She/Itwas not taking
Wewere not taking
Youwere not taking
Theywere not taking

Verb => take

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Examples

  • I was not watching the television.
  • She was not listening to music.
  • We were not going to the party.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Examples

  • Was he playing football?

  • Was she sleeping?
  • Were they coming?

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Actions at a Specific Time

  • Used to describe actions that were in progress at a precise time in the past.

Examples

  • She was writing an email at 8 PM last night.
  • They were talking on the phone at noon.
  • We were talking to each other during lunch.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Interrupted Actions

  • Used for actions that were happening when another action (usually Past Simple) interrupted them.

Examples

  • I was staying with my friend when you called.
  • She was working as a cashier when the store closed.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Parallel Actions (Two things at once)

  • Used when two or more actions were happening at the same time in the past.

Examples

  • We were travelling to Paris while it was raining.
  • He was meeting his boss while I was working.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Atmosphere & Background

  • Used to describe the atmosphere or introduction in a story.

Examples

  • The birds were singing and the sun was shining.
  • The wind was blowing hard that night.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Spelling Rules for Present Participle (-ing)

Base Verb Rule Example
Most verbs Add -ing work → working
Verbs ending in -e Drop 'e', add -ing write → writing
One vowel + one consonant Double consonant run → running
Verbs ending in -ie Change 'ie' to 'y', add -ing die → dying

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Time Expressions

  • While, when, as
  • All day, all morning, the whole night
  • At [time] yesterday (e.g., At 5 PM yesterday)

Examples

  • She was watching TV all evening.
  • They were working hard when the boss arrived.
  • We were going to the concert when the car broke down.

PAST CONTINUOUS TENSE

Comparing Past Continuous & Past Simple

Past Continuous Past Simple
Action in progress Completed action
'She was reading when I entered.' 'She read the book yesterday.'
Background action/Scene Main event/Interruption
'The sun was shining...' '...suddenly, it started to rain.'

PAST SIMPLE TENSE

Comparing Past Simple & Past Continuous

Past Simple Past Continuous
Completed Action Action in Progress
Describes an action that started and finished at a specific time in the past. Describes an action that was happening at a specific moment in the past.
'I watched a movie yesterday.' 'I was watching a movie when you called.'
Sequence of Events Simultaneous Events
'He came in, took off his coat, and sat down.' 'He was cooking while she was reading.'
